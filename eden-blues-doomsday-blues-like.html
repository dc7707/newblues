Eden Blues / Doomsday Blues-like

Idea: A game loosely based on the concepts and mechanics in Eden Blues (AKA Doomsday Blues) that was played by BillBull.

Link: https://www.mobygames.com/game/6267/eden-blues/

Manual: https://cpcrulez.fr/f/35910

BillBull Vod: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e99TMQx403k

Description:

Moby Games: In this real-time adventure you are a prisoner of jail guarded by droids. And you know the roster of their duty, some of them are on beat by day, some by night. Another of them like a dogs, who is on beat all time. But you have to find the way out of labyrinth using your legs and a knack to bash the doors out. With time your health is decreasing and you must find the bottles to recuperate. When you are bashing the door, your physical endurance is decreasing and you must find a food to restore it. In game you'll also find the coins for coffee dispensing machines but beware of talking portraits and other traps...

Me: This game is about exploration, maze navigation, and resource management. You need to find the path through a large prison full of traps, enemies, and obstacles, all while keeping your health and strength up via items you can find hidden along the way.

Items / Objects:

DOORS: Opened by a fierce kick. The force required is a function of the value shown underneath the window on the status table.
BOTTLES: Increase your hero's FITNESS level. (Bottles can be found in the cellars.)
PLATES (of food): Increase your hero's STRENGTH.
CUPS OF COFFEE: Increase his COURAGE. Each cup costs a IF coin.
IF COIN: Needed to get coffee from the vending machines. (Coins are sometimes found on the window ledges in cells.)

Liquor Cabinet: Using increases your Bottles number.

Tables of Food: Using increases your Strength number.

Characteristics (Stats):

What I Like:

  • This game reminds me of the Spy Vs Spy game I played on NES. While that is a player vs player, or NPC acting like a player, this has an objective and a slew of enemies and hazards. SvS elements are finding objects in rooms, trapped areas, and general vibe. (Maybe I can expand on other reasons later, but it's just a feeling,)
  • Exploration and day / night cycles are important to finding your way out.
  • It's just a bit weird. (Maybe expand on this)

What I'd Change or Add (If I could):

  • There isn't much point to combat. You are much better off completely avoiding it. Could this be balanced and incentivized? Is it better to just fully remove it? Maybe a simplified use item to kill one enemy instead?
  • If pursuing combat, a  much more reliable system is needed. In EB it seemed very risky and hard to pin down strategy.
  • Do I want there to be a time limit?
  • Waiting for day/night cycles is tedious. 
  • No damage from walking into walls (WTF)
  • I can't confirm this without a lot of testing, but it seems there are maybe one to three at most paths that are possible -- it may just be one.
  • Too much resource management? Ties into above point a bit.
  • Possibly work in some sort of Spy Vs Spy like trap(s) you can set for enemies
  • Romance options (LOL)

Things I'd Like to Do But Probably Can't:

  • Make it rogue-like for replayability
  • Music (I suck at this more than other stuff)
  • Customization options (build your own map etc)
